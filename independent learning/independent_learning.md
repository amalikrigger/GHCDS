# 📚 Independent Learning Study

### Pick a skill, set a goal, prove you learned it

> **Grades:** 7th–12th &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Time:** Ongoing (throughout the trimester) &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Points:** 50
>
> This is YOUR chance to learn something that interests you — at your own pace, on your own path. You'll pick a topic, set a realistic goal, and work toward it each week. The only rule: you have to actually learn, not just click through.

---

## 🎯 How It Works

1. **Pick an option** from the list below (or propose your own)
2. **Set your goal** — how far will you get by the end of the trimester?
3. **Get it approved** by Mr. Krigger (we'll agree on your target together)
4. **Work on it** during independent work time
5. **Check in weekly** — show your progress every Friday
6. **Submit proof** at the end — if you hit your goal, you get full credit

> **The deal:** You tell me what percentage of a course you'll complete. If you say 50% and you do 50%, that's full credit. If you say 50% and only do 30%, that's partial credit. Be honest with yourself — it's better to set a realistic goal and hit it than to overshoot and fall short.

## 📊 Setting Your Goal

This is the most important part. You need to be honest with yourself.

### For Online Courses:

Look at the course and count the total units/sections/lessons. Then decide what percentage you can realistically complete this trimester.

| Goal Level | What It Means | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **25%** | A quarter of the course | Good if you're busy with other classes or this is very challenging material |
| **50%** | Half the course | A solid, reasonable target for most students |
| **75%** | Three-quarters | Ambitious — you'll need to work consistently |
| **100%** | The whole thing | Very ambitious — only set this if you're confident and motivated |

### For Hands-On Specialties:

Your goal will be defined by deliverables (e.g., "complete the guide + 1 print" or "finish 15 of 20 tutorials + build 2 projects").

### How Grading Works:

| You Hit... | Grade |
|---|---|
| 100% of your goal | Full credit (50/50) |
| 75-99% of your goal | 40/50 |
| 50-74% of your goal | 30/50 |
| 25-49% of your goal | 20/50 |
| Less than 25% | 10/50 |
| Didn't start or no evidence | 0/50 |

**+1 extra credit point** for every additional unit/section completed beyond your goal.

## 📅 Weekly Check-Ins (Every Friday)

Every Friday, you'll do a 2-minute check-in:

1. **Show your screen** — where are you in the course right now?
2. **Tell me one thing you learned this week** — one sentence is fine
3. **Any blockers?** — are you stuck on anything?

This isn't a quiz — it's just to make sure you're on track so there are no surprises at the end.

> **If you're falling behind your goal:** That's okay! We can adjust your target mid-trimester. It's better to adjust early than to give up. Talk to me.

## 📝 What to Submit

### At the Start (Week 1):
Fill out the **Independent Learning Contract** (separate document) and submit it on Schoology. This locks in your choice and goal.

### Weekly:
Friday check-ins (in person, no submission needed — Mr. Krigger will track it).

### At the End:
Submit on Schoology:

1. **Progress screenshot** — your course dashboard or completion page showing exactly where you finished
2. **Proof of learning** — this depends on your option:
   - Online course: screenshot of completion percentage + 1 paragraph about what you learned
   - Hands-on specialty: your deliverable (guide, models, projects) + photos
   - Custom path: whatever evidence we agreed on in your contract
3. **Reflection** (3-5 sentences): What was the most valuable thing you learned? Would you continue this on your own? What would you do differently?

> **⚠️ When submitting in Schoology:** Click **"Upload"** — do **NOT** click "Create." Create is for text only and won't let you attach files. You must use Upload to submit screenshots and media.

> **Need help taking screenshots?** See the [How to Take & Submit Screenshots](../fundamentals/how_to_screenshot.md) guide.

## 💡 Tips for Success

- **Pick something you're genuinely curious about.** You'll be spending weeks on this — boredom kills motivation.
- **Set a goal you'll actually hit.** 50% completed is infinitely better than 100% planned and 20% done.
- **Do a little each week.** 20 minutes twice a week beats a 3-hour panic session the night before it's due.
- **If you're stuck, ask.** Don't sit there confused for a week. That's wasted time.
- **The goal isn't to rush through lessons.** It's to actually understand the material. Quality over speed.
