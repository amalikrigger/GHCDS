# 🖨️ 3D Printing: Design, Print, and Teach

### Find or create a 3D model, print it, and make a guide showing others how

> **Grades:** 7th–12th &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Time:** 2-3 class periods &nbsp;|&nbsp; **Difficulty:** Beginner
>
> You'll find or design a 3D model, print it on one of our classroom printers (MakerBot or Formlabs), and create a PowerPoint presentation that teaches someone else how to use the printer you chose. By the end, you'll have a real object you made and a guide others can follow.

---

## 🎯 What You'll Learn

- How to find or design a 3D model
- How to prepare a model for printing (slicing)
- How to operate a **MakerBot** or **Formlabs** 3D printer
- How to document a process clearly enough that someone else can follow it

---

## 🧰 What You Need

- Access to a classroom 3D printer (**MakerBot** or **Formlabs**)
- A computer with a web browser
- A personal email address (for creating accounts)

---

# Part 1: Find or Design Your Model

You have two options — pick a model from the internet or design your own.

---

### Option A — Download a Model

Browse one of these sites and find something you want to print:

| Site | Link | Good For |
|---|---|---|
| **Thingiverse** | [thingiverse.com](https://www.thingiverse.com/) | Huge library, popular designs, beginner-friendly |
| **Printables** | [printables.com](https://www.printables.com/) | High-quality models, good search filters |

**Tips for choosing:**
- Look for models labeled "easy" or "beginner"
- Check the estimated print time — **keep it under 60 minutes**
- Download the `.STL` file (that's the standard 3D printing format)
- Avoid models with lots of tiny details — they're harder to print

---

### Option B — Design Your Own

Create something original using one of these free tools:

| Tool | Link | Good For |
|---|---|---|
| **Tinkercad** | [tinkercad.com](https://www.tinkercad.com/) | Beginners — drag and drop shapes, very easy |
| **AutoCAD** | [autodesk.com](https://www.autodesk.com/) | More advanced — precise engineering designs |
| Any other 3D modeling tool | Your choice | If you already know one, use it! |

**If using Tinkercad:**
1. Go to [tinkercad.com](https://www.tinkercad.com/) and create a free account
2. Click **Create new design**
3. Drag shapes onto the workspace and combine them
4. When done, click **Export** → download as `.STL`

> **✅ Checkpoint:** You have an `.STL` file ready to print — either downloaded or exported from your design tool.

---

# Part 2: Print Your Model

You'll use either the **MakerBot** or **Formlabs** printer. Pick one and follow the steps below.

---

### If Using the MakerBot Printer

#### Step 1 — Create an Ultimaker Account

You need a free account to use the Ultimaker Digital Factory (this is where you prepare your print files).

1. Go to [account.ultimaker.com](https://account.ultimaker.com)
2. Click **Create account**
3. **Use your personal email** (not your school email — school emails often block the verification)
4. Verify your email and log in
5. You'll land on the **Ultimaker Digital Factory** dashboard

> **✅ Checkpoint:** You're logged into Ultimaker Digital Factory and can see the dashboard.

---

#### Step 2 — Prepare Your Print File

1. In Ultimaker Digital Factory, click **Print Preparation**
2. Select the 3D printer — choose **Replicator+** (or whatever model matches your classroom printer)
3. Click **Import** and upload your `.STL` file
4. Your model will appear on the virtual build plate — you can rotate and reposition it
5. Choose your settings:
   - **Material:** PLA
   - **Quality:** Standard (or Draft for faster prints)
   - **Support:** Turn on if your model has overhangs
6. Check the estimated print time — **must be under 60 minutes**
7. Click **Export** to generate the `.makerbot` file
8. **Save the file to a USB flash drive**

> **What just happened?** The software "sliced" your 3D model into hundreds of thin layers and created instructions the printer can follow, layer by layer. That's what the `.makerbot` file contains.

---

#### Step 3 — Load Filament (if needed)

If the printer doesn't have filament loaded, or you need to switch colors:

**To unload the current filament:**
1. On the printer's screen, tap **Filament**
2. Tap **Unload Filament**
3. Wait for the printer to heat up (it will tell you when it's ready)
4. When prompted, gently pull the filament out of the **extruder** (the part at the top)
5. Pull the filament out of the **tube**
6. Remove the filament spool from the **holder** on the side

**To load new filament:**
1. On the printer's screen, tap **Filament**
2. Tap **Load Filament**
3. Wait for the printer to heat up
4. Place the new filament spool on the **holder**
5. Feed the filament into the **tube**
6. Push the filament into the **extruder** — push firmly until the extruder grabs it and starts pulling it in
7. You'll see filament ooze out of the nozzle — that means it's loaded!
8. Tap **Done** on the screen

> **Tip:** If the extruder won't grab the filament, push a little harder. Sometimes you need to squeeze the release button on the side of the extruder to help guide it in.

---

#### Step 4 — Print!

1. Plug your **USB flash drive** into the printer
2. On the printer's home screen, tap **Print**
3. Tap **USB Storage**
4. Find and tap your `.makerbot` file
5. Tap **Print**
6. Watch the first few layers to make sure the filament is sticking to the build plate
7. Don't touch the printer while it's running!

---

#### Step 5 — Remove Your Print

1. Wait for the print to finish completely
2. Let the build plate cool down (a few minutes)
3. Gently remove your print from the plate (use the scraper tool if needed — be careful!)
4. Remove any support material if you used supports

---

### If Using the Formlabs Printer

**Step 1 — Prepare Your Model**

1. Open the Formlabs software (PreForm) on the classroom computer
2. Import your `.STL` file
3. The software will suggest orientation and supports — accept the defaults for now
4. Check the estimated print time — **must be under 60 minutes**

**Step 2 — Print**

1. Send the job to the Formlabs printer
2. Make sure the resin tank has enough resin
3. Start the print
4. Wait for it to finish

**Step 3 — Post-Processing**

Formlabs prints need extra steps after printing:

1. **Wash** the print in the wash station (removes excess resin)
2. **Cure** the print under UV light (hardens the resin)
3. **Remove supports** carefully with flush cutters

> **Formlabs prints look smoother** but require more post-processing. MakerBot prints are simpler to handle but have visible layer lines.

---

**Take a photo of your finished print!** You'll need it for your deliverables.

> **✅ Checkpoint:** You have a finished 3D print and a photo of it.

---

# Part 3: Create Your Printer Guide (PowerPoint)

Now teach someone else how to use the printer you chose.

---

### What Your PowerPoint Should Include

Create a presentation (in **PowerPoint, Google Slides, or Canva**) that walks someone through the entire process of printing on **the printer you used** (MakerBot OR Formlabs — pick one).

**Required slides:**

| Slide | Content |
|---|---|
| **Title slide** | "How to Print on [MakerBot / Formlabs]" + your name |
| **What you need** | List the materials, software, accounts, and supplies required |
| **Preparing the file** | How to get from an STL file to a printer-ready file (MakerBot: Ultimaker Digital Factory → export `.makerbot` → USB. Formlabs: PreForm software → send to printer) |
| **Loading filament/resin** | Step-by-step with photos of how to load material into the printer (include unloading too for MakerBot) |
| **Printing** | How to start the print, what to watch for in the first few layers |
| **Removing the print** | How to safely remove the finished print (MakerBot: cool plate + scraper. Formlabs: wash + cure + remove supports) |
| **Tips and warnings** | Common mistakes, what NOT to do, helpful hints |
| **Your print** | Photo of your finished print and what you learned |

**What makes a great guide:**
- **Screenshots or photos** of each step (take them while you're printing!)
- **Short, clear text** — someone should be able to follow it without asking questions
- **Numbered steps** — so the order is obvious
- **Warnings** about common mistakes (like not waiting for the plate to cool)

> **Think of it this way:** If a student who has never used a 3D printer reads your guide, could they print something successfully? That's the goal.

## 🧠 Vocabulary to Know

| Term | What It Means |
|---|---|
| **STL File** | The standard file format for 3D printing — it describes the shape of your model |
| **Slicing** | The software process that converts your 3D model into instructions the printer can follow |
| **PLA** | A common 3D printing material (plastic filament) used in MakerBot printers |
| **Resin** | A liquid material used in Formlabs printers — hardens when exposed to UV light |
| **Build Plate** | The flat surface the printer builds your object on |
| **Supports** | Temporary structures printed to hold up overhanging parts — removed after printing |
| **Post-Processing** | Steps after printing: washing, curing, removing supports |
| **Overhang** | A part of the model that sticks out with nothing below it — needs supports |

## 💡 Troubleshooting

**"My print isn't sticking to the build plate"**
→ Make sure the build plate is clean and level. For MakerBot, try re-leveling the plate in settings. Wipe the plate with rubbing alcohol to remove oils.

**"The print time is over 60 minutes"**
→ Scale your model down (make it smaller), reduce the quality to "draft," or choose a simpler design.

**"My model looks weird in the slicer software"**
→ The STL file might be corrupted. Try re-downloading it or re-exporting from Tinkercad.

**"The print has messy strings between parts"**
→ This is called "stringing." You can carefully remove strings with tweezers or a craft knife. It's normal and doesn't mean the print failed.

**"I can't create an Ultimaker account"**
→ Try using your personal email instead of your school email. Check your spam folder for the verification email.

**"The printer says no filament detected"**
→ The filament may not be fully seated in the extruder. Unload and reload it, pushing firmly until the extruder grabs it and filament oozes from the nozzle.

**"The USB drive doesn't show any files"**
→ Make sure you exported the `.makerbot` file (not just the `.STL`) and saved it to the root of the USB drive, not inside a folder.

**"The filament won't go into the extruder"**
→ Make sure the printer has heated up first (wait for the prompt). Try cutting the tip of the filament at an angle to make it easier to feed in. Squeeze the release lever on the side while pushing.
