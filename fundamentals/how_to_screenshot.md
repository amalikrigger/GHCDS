# 📸 How to Take & Submit Screenshots

### A quick guide for capturing your screen and uploading to Schoology

---

## Taking a Screenshot

### On a Mac

| What You Want | Shortcut | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| **Entire screen** | **Cmd + Shift + 3** | Captures everything on your screen |
| **Selected area** | **Cmd + Shift + 4** | Lets you drag a box around what you want to capture |
| **A single window** | **Cmd + Shift + 4**, then press **Spacebar** | Click the window you want to capture |

Screenshots save to your **Desktop** by default.

---

### On a Windows PC

| What You Want | Shortcut | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| **Entire screen** | **Windows + Print Screen** | Captures the full screen, saves to Pictures > Screenshots |
| **Selected area** | **Windows + Shift + S** | Opens Snip & Sketch — drag a box around what you want |
| **A single window** | **Alt + Print Screen** | Captures the active window to your clipboard (paste into a doc) |

> **Tip:** If you use **Windows + Shift + S**, the screenshot copies to your clipboard. Open Paint, Word, or any app and press **Ctrl + V** to paste it. Then save the file.

---

### On a Chromebook

| What You Want | Shortcut | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| **Entire screen** | **Ctrl + Show Windows** (the rectangle key with lines) | Captures the full screen |
| **Selected area** | **Ctrl + Shift + Show Windows** | Lets you drag a box around what you want |

Screenshots save to your **Downloads** folder.

---

### On a Phone or Tablet

| Device | How to Screenshot |
|---|---|
| **iPhone / iPad** | Press **Side Button + Volume Up** at the same time |
| **Android** | Press **Power + Volume Down** at the same time |

Screenshots save to your **Photos** app.

---

### On a Raspberry Pi

| What You Want | How |
|---|---|
| **Entire screen** | Press the **Print Screen** key on your keyboard |
| **Using the terminal** | Run: `scrot ~/Desktop/screenshot.png` |
| **Delayed screenshot** | Run: `scrot -d 5 ~/Desktop/screenshot.png` (waits 5 seconds) |

> **Tip:** If `scrot` isn't installed, run `sudo apt install scrot` first.

---

## Submitting Screenshots in Schoology

### Step-by-Step

1. In Schoology, find the assignment and click on it
2. Click the **Submit Assignment** button
3. **Click "Upload"** (the upload icon / file upload option)
4. Select your screenshot file(s) from your computer
5. Click **Submit**

> **⚠️ Important: Use "Upload", NOT "Create."** The "Create" option is for typing text — it does not let you attach image files. You must use the **Upload** button to submit screenshots and media files.

### File Tips

- **Accepted formats:** PNG, JPG, JPEG, GIF, PDF
- **File naming:** Name your files clearly so your teacher knows what they are (e.g., `day1_dashboard_screenshot.png`, `ssh_terminal.png`)
- **Multiple files:** You can upload multiple screenshots at once — just select them all before clicking Submit
- If you took the screenshot on your phone, you can email it to yourself, AirDrop it, or upload it to Google Drive first, then download it on your computer to submit
